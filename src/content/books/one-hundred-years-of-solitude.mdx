## Why We Chose This Book

García Márquez passed away in 2014, and the tenth anniversary seemed right to celebrate his masterwork. This Nobel Prize winner is often called one of the most important novels of the 20th century—we wanted to see if it lived up to the hype.

<ReadingNote title="Keeping Characters Straight">
Many members found a family tree essential. The repeating names (José Arcadio, Aureliano, Remedios) are intentional—they reflect the cyclical nature of the family's fate. We created a shared document to track them all!
</ReadingNote>

## Reading Companion

### The Buendía Family (Abbreviated!)

- **José Arcadio Buendía** - The founder, obsessed with science and invention
- **Úrsula Iguarán** - The matriarch who holds everything together
- **Colonel Aureliano Buendía** - Who made 32 wars and lost them all
- **Remedios the Beauty** - Too beautiful for this earth (literally)
- **Amaranta** - Whose bitterness spans generations

### Historical Context

The novel spans roughly 1820-1920 in Colombian history, including:
- The founding of inland towns
- The Liberal/Conservative civil wars
- The arrival of the banana companies
- The massacre of striking workers in 1928

<Spoiler>
The ending reveals that Melquíades's parchments—which the family has tried to decipher for generations—contain the entire history of the Buendías, written in advance. As Aureliano reads, Macondo is destroyed by wind. The family's story was always predetermined, their solitude inescapable. It's one of literature's most devastating conclusions.
</Spoiler>

## Spanish-Language Discussion

Several bilingual members read the original Spanish. They noted that García Márquez's prose is more musical in Spanish, with longer sentences that create a trance-like reading experience.
