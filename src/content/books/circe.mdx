## About This Selection

*Circe* was our November 2024 selection, continuing our exploration of mythological retellings. Madeline Miller's lyrical prose brought new depth to a figure often reduced to a minor villain in The Odyssey.

## Why We Chose This Book

After the success of *The Song of Achilles* in our 2023 readings, members voted overwhelmingly to return to Miller's work. Circe offered a different perspective—that of an immortal outsider finding her own power.

<ReadingNote title="Mythological Sources">
Miller draws from Homer's Odyssey, Ovid's Metamorphoses, and various Greek texts. For those interested, the Homeric Hymns provide additional context for the gods' characterizations.
</ReadingNote>

## Discussion Highlights

November's meeting ran an extra hour because no one wanted to stop talking! Key points included:

1. **Circe's Evolution** - From rejected nymph to powerful witch to devoted mother
2. **The Gods' Cruelty** - How Miller portrays divine indifference
3. **Odysseus's Complexity** - Neither hero nor villain, but something more human
4. **The Island as Character** - Aiaia as both prison and sanctuary

## Reading Companion

### Key Characters

- **Circe** - Daughter of Helios, goddess of witchcraft
- **Helios** - Titan of the Sun, Circe's father
- **Odysseus** - The cunning Greek hero
- **Telegonus** - Circe's son by Odysseus
- **Athena** - Gray-eyed goddess, Odysseus's patron

### Mythological Context

Understanding the broader Greek mythological framework enriches the reading. Circe interacts with many famous figures: Prometheus, Daedalus, Medea, the Minotaur, Scylla, and more.

<Spoiler>
The ending, where Circe chooses mortality to be with Telemachus, inverts traditional Greek values where immortality was the ultimate prize. Miller suggests that limitation—having an ending—is what makes choices meaningful.
</Spoiler>
