## Why We Chose This Book

Many developers struggle with design, often falling back on frameworks and templates. This book provides concrete tactics that anyone can apply to immediately improve their interfaces, making it perfect for our technically-minded club members.

<ReadingNote title="Systems Over Inspiration">
The authors advocate for systematic approaches to design—predefined spacing scales, consistent color palettes, and typography hierarchies—rather than making decisions on a case-by-case basis.
</ReadingNote>

## Reading Companion

### Core Principles

- **Start with too much white space** - Then reduce, rather than starting cramped
- **Establish clear hierarchy** - Size, weight, and color all contribute
- **Design in grayscale first** - Then add color for meaning
- **Limit your choices** - Constraints breed creativity
- **Steal like an artist** - Study designs you admire

### The Spacing Scale

The authors recommend defining a spacing scale upfront:

- 4px, 8px, 12px, 16px, 24px, 32px, 48px, 64px, 96px, 128px

This constraint makes spacing decisions faster and more consistent.

<Spoiler>
One of the book's most powerful insights is that "good" design often means removing elements rather than adding them. The cleanest interfaces achieve their goals with the minimum necessary visual complexity.
</Spoiler>

## Practical Exercises

Try these techniques from the book:

- Redesign a form using only font size and weight for hierarchy (no color)
- Build a complete color palette from a single base color
- Create a component library with predefined spacing values
- Analyze your favorite app's visual hierarchy
