## About This Selection

*Pachinko* was our June 2024 selection—an epic that follows four generations of a Korean family in Japan. Min Jin Lee spent thirty years crafting this sweeping tale of identity, discrimination, and resilience.

## Why We Chose This Book

Several members had watched the Apple TV+ adaptation and wanted to experience the source material. The novel offered even more depth than the show, spanning from 1910 to 1989.

<ReadingNote title="Historical Background">
Korea was under Japanese occupation from 1910-1945. Koreans who moved to Japan faced (and continue to face) significant discrimination. The "Zainichi" Koreans occupy a complex social position that the novel illuminates beautifully.
</ReadingNote>

## Reading Companion

### Key Characters Across Generations

**First Generation:**
- **Sunja** - The heart of the novel
- **Isak** - The minister who gives Sunja a future

**Second Generation:**
- **Noa** - The brilliant, tortured son
- **Mozasu** - The practical pachinko businessman

**Third Generation:**
- **Solomon** - Caught between cultures in modern Japan

### Historical Events

- 1910: Japanese annexation of Korea
- 1923: Great Kanto Earthquake (and anti-Korean violence)
- 1939-1945: World War II
- 1950-1953: Korean War
- Post-war: Economic recovery and discrimination

<Spoiler>
Noa's suicide is the novel's emotional center. His discovery that Hansu—a yakuza boss—is his biological father shatters his carefully constructed "Japanese" identity. He spends years hiding, only to take his own life when discovered. Lee suggests that the pressure to assimilate can be as destructive as overt discrimination.
</Spoiler>

## TV Adaptation

The Apple TV+ series (2022-present) takes creative liberties but captures the novel's spirit. Many members enjoyed both while appreciating their differences.
